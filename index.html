Myth vs Fact

Clearing common misconceptions about the electoral process.

Myth: My vote doesn't really matter.

Fact: Every single vote counts! Many local and national elections have been decided by incredibly narrow margins. Your vote is your voice in shaping the future.

Myth: I can vote with just my Aadhaar card even if I'm not registered.

Fact: No, you MUST be registered on the electoral roll. An ID card like Aadhaar or Voter ID is used to verify your identity at the polling station, but it does not replace the requirement of being registered.

Myth: Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs) can be hacked through the internet.

Fact: EVMs are standalone, non-networked devices. They do not have any wireless communication capabilities like Wi-Fi or Bluetooth, making remote hacking impossible.

Question: What is NOTA?

Answer: NOTA stands for "None of the Above". It is an option on the EVM that allows a voter to officially register a vote of rejection for all candidates contesting in that constituency.

Question: Can Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) vote?

Answer: Yes, Indian citizens living abroad who have not acquired citizenship of any other country are eligible to be registered as voters in their home constituency. They need to fill Form 6A.
